From the Book

When the memoir continues in sound

Music has always been more than a backdrop in my life. In many moments, it was refuge, company, strength, and, at times, the only way to understand what I was feeling when words still did not exist. Throughout this story, music was present in a quiet but constant way — on the radio, in my headphones, in the lyrics that stayed with me while I studied, dreamed, suffered, fell in love, and tried to find my place in the world.

Some songs made me laugh. Others made me cry. Some gave me energy to prepare for the ITA entrance exam, some inspired me when everything seemed difficult, and some always brought me back to the love of my life — the girl of my dreams. This book tells my story in words. But there are emotions that never fully fit inside them. That is why I decided to share two playlists that are an essential part of this journey. They are not random lists. Each song carries a moment, a feeling, or a memory that helped shape who I became.

You do not need to listen to these songs to understand this story. The book stands on its own. But if you want, you can return to a few chapters, put on your headphones, and let the music accompany the reading. At certain moments, it says what I could not say. This is my story in words. What follows is the continuation in sound.

Playlist 2 — The Night When Anyone Can Step Onto the Stage

This second playlist is connected to a very specific moment in my life — one of the most emotional nights I have ever lived. It was the night of the Kayak band performance during the CTA June festival. An improvised stage, a blend of musicians from the Northeast, different stories, and dreams that until then had seemed unlikely. In front of hundreds of people, we sang, played, and lived something unforgettable.

Not because anyone there was a famous artist, but because, in that moment, it became clear that it was not necessary to be one. Many people like to sing but never do. They believe they do not have a voice, that they will be compared to professional singers, that they will be judged or ridiculed. Few realize that what we hear on records involves technique, equipment, production, and support — and that music, before anything else, is human expression.

Being on that stage meant understanding something simple and powerful: it was not about exceptional talent, but about ordinary courage. That night proved that a hidden dream, seemingly distant, can become real. That anyone can step onto the stage — whether literal or symbolic. This playlist is the soundtrack of that night and of everything it represented.
