Creative Life

Music, Voice, and Creative Expression

Music is not an accessory in Roberto’s life. It is one of the languages through which memory, love, longing, courage, hope, and gratitude have been lived and understood. Across the book, the recordings, and the personal reflections behind each song, music appears as refuge, study companion, emotional archive, family bond, and stage for improbable dreams.

Some songs are tied to decisive chapters of life. “Halo” carries the joy of recording with his daughter Gabriela. “When Your Feet Don’t Touch the Ground” marks the gift of recording with his daughter Rebecca. “The Promise” became a collaboration with his brother Marcelo. ABBA’s “Dancing Queen” reaches back to the first awakening of his love for music in 1978. “How Can I Go On” became a mirror for the impossible dream of reaching a top engineering college in Brazil. Axé and forró opened the door to singing on stage before hundreds of people and realizing a lifelong dream.

From the Book

When the memoir continues in sound

The closing pages of Impossible Crossing turn openly toward music and playlist. The soundtrack section gathers songs that made Roberto laugh, cry, and believe, tying them to specific chapters and emotional phases of the memoir.

Those final pages are not decorative. They show that some chapters are completed not only in prose, but in sound — with Queen, ABBA, Air Supply, Elton John, Maria Bethânia, Paul McCartney, Stevie Wonder, Tears for Fears, and others extending the emotional progression of the book beyond its last narrative pages.

Playlist as emotional map

The soundtrack follows the movement of the memoir chapter by chapter, from childhood musical discovery to perseverance, love, courage, and self-expression.

A second playlist about courage

The book closes with the night when anyone can step onto a stage: a memory of singing with a band before hundreds of people and discovering that music is not about celebrity, but about ordinary courage.

Family, Memory, and Milestones

Selected musical anchors

Recording with family

“Halo” carries the joy of recording with Gabriela. “When Your Feet Don’t Touch the Ground” carries the joy of recording with Rebecca. “The Promise” preserves a duet with Marcelo and the gratitude of seeing impossible-looking dreams become real.

Songs that formed a life

“Dancing Queen” points back to 1978 and the first spark of musical love. “How Can I Go On” became part of the inner dialogue of perseverance. Air Supply became motivation during the years when others treated the dream as unattainable.

The stage dream

Axé and forró led to a defining moment: singing in front of hundreds of people with a college band and realizing that music, before anything else, is human expression and courage.

Video and Audio

Yes, audio can live on a GitHub-hosted site

You can absolutely host audio on a GitHub Pages website. The simplest approach is to place MP3 files inside a folder such as audio/ and use the standard HTML <audio controls> player. You can also link to a YouTube performance once you choose the featured video for this page.

A good next step is to feature one meaningful video, then add a small set of selected recordings below it rather than everything at once.
