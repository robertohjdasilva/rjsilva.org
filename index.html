Engineering with a Heart

This is both memoir and manifesto: a space where technology, resilience, ethics, and human dignity meet. It grows out of a life lived between different worlds — Brazil and the United States, struggle and opportunity, invention and responsibility.

Engineering

Distributed systems, networking, observability, architecture, troubleshooting, and the invisible machinery behind modern life.

Humanity

A reminder that behind every system there are people: families, workers, students, communities, and lives shaped by technology.

Purpose

Reflections on what progress means when measured not only by efficiency, but by fairness, empathy, and dignity.

Impossible Crossing

Impossible Crossing is an integral part of this project. It tells the story of a life shaped by struggle, perseverance, education, migration, and purpose.

From washing a muddy truck in the Amazon to becoming a principal engineer in the United States, the book provides the human foundation for everything this site explores.

About R.J. Silva

R.J. Silva is a Brazilian-born engineer, inventor, teacher, and storyteller. His work spans distributed systems, networking, observability, and software architecture, while his writing explores technology, humanity, and service.

He is the author of Impossible Crossing and the creator of Engineering with a Heart.

“The most beautiful systems are invisible. When they work, people don’t think about them — they simply live better because of them.”
