A human crossing through hardship, belonging, hope, and purpose

Impossible Crossing is an autobiographical work of unusual emotional maturity. It does not present life as a neat ascent toward success. Instead, it follows a long crossing shaped by poverty, bullying, education, migration, faith, perseverance, love, and the many people whose generosity changed one life without fanfare.

What makes the book powerful is not just what happens, but how the world is observed. It looks at people through the best they were able to offer, without denying pain, injustice, ambiguity, or loss. That ethical gaze gives the memoir unusual dignity and lasting force.

At its center, this is a book about believing: believing in people, in meaning, in gratitude, and in the possibility that even an impossible crossing can be crossed.

Foreword

Nicole Neuman on courage, humanity, and resonance

“Its power does not come from noise or spectacle, but from the way courage becomes intimate and contagious.”

The foreword helps frame the memoir as something more enduring than a dramatic life story. It recognizes a narrative marked by gratitude rather than resentment, intimacy rather than posture, and a rare trust in the reader’s intelligence.

That perspective matters because it identifies what makes the book distinctive: it is generous without naivety, emotionally strong without self-pity, and deeply rooted in ordinary people whose gestures quietly alter destinies.

Prologue

The airplane as mystery, rupture, departure, and hope

The prologue opens with an airplane image that becomes more than a childhood memory. It becomes a symbol of separation, survival, migration, return, and the opening of worlds that once felt unreachable.

From the first pages, the memoir announces its deeper movement: this is not the story of an idealized hero, but of an ordinary person carried forward by faith, perseverance, unexpected help, and the human encounters that made each next step possible.

It is an elegant beginning because it compresses the whole arc of the book into one image: leaving the ground without certainty and discovering that life-changing crossings often begin before we understand where they lead.

“Almost every major change in my life began like this: with the airplane leaving the ground.”

That image also anticipates the structure of the memoir itself: a story of movement not only across geography, but across class, language, belonging, identity, and hope.

Selected Highlights

Three dimensions of the memoir

Beginning

An impossible crossing

The early chapters reveal a life shaped by fragility, separation, hardship, and social vulnerability. Yet even there, the memoir focuses on the people whose presence made survival, dignity, and growth possible.

Formation

Education, discipline, and perseverance

Across school years, bullying, failure, ambition, and improbable goals, the book follows the long formation of character. The emotional center is never prestige alone, but the stubborn decision to keep believing in a future not yet visible.

Expression

Music as refuge, courage, and memory

In the later chapters, music becomes more than soundtrack. Songs become emotional anchors — holding together pain, joy, longing, romantic memory, parental love, study, courage, and self-discovery.

Final Pages

When the story continues in sound

The closing pages make an important move: they openly state that music has always been more than background. It was refuge, companionship, motivation, and sometimes the only way to understand feelings before words could fully carry them.

Those pages end in curated playlists tied to specific chapters. Queen, ABBA, Air Supply, Elton John, Maria Bethânia, Paul McCartney, Stevie Wonder, Tears for Fears, and others are not used decoratively; they map the emotional progression of the memoir and extend it beyond the page.

This section matters because it reveals one of the book’s deepest truths: endurance is built not only through sacrifice and discipline, but also through beauty, melody, memory, and the songs that help a person keep going.

Why these pages stand out

  • They show that the book’s emotional life continues after the last chapter.
  • They connect biography to a broader cultural soundtrack.
  • They prepare the reader to see the author not only as engineer and writer, but also as singer.
  • They make the memoir feel even more intimate, lived, and memorable.
Author

About R. J. Silva

R. J. Silva is a software engineer, inventor, writer, and singer with 29 U.S. patents. Born in Brazil, he later lived and worked in Europe and the United States, carrying those crossings into both his technical work and creative life.

His path never moved away from people or from the inner life that stories and songs preserve. Impossible Crossing was born from a desire to honor the people, encounters, and invisible acts of generosity that changed a destiny.

Beyond the Book

Part of a larger creative project

The memoir is one pillar of a broader ecosystem that includes Engineering with a Heart, essays, music, and public reflection. The book tells the life story; the site extends that voice into technology, ethics, resilience, and creative expression.
