Introduction

About Roberto H. Jacob da Silva

Roberto H. Jacob da Silva is a Brazilian-born engineer, inventor, author, and storyteller whose work connects deep technical systems with human meaning. Over a career of more than two decades, he has designed and led work in distributed systems, networking, virtualization, cloud infrastructure, observability, and diagnostics.

His professional path includes major engineering roles at Microsoft, Lenovo, IBM, and Nokia, along with a long record of innovation recognized through 29 U.S. patents. At the same time, his writing has remained rooted in a different but equally important question: how can knowledge, technology, and opportunity become more human, more understandable, and more useful to others?

“Technology matters most when it helps us see one another more clearly, serve one another more faithfully, and build with both excellence and compassion.”

Journey

A bridge across worlds

Having lived in Brazil, Spain, Belgium, and the United States, Roberto writes from the perspective of someone who has crossed different cultures, languages, and realities. That journey shapes both his memoir and his public writing: not as a celebration of achievement for its own sake, but as an expression of gratitude for the people, sacrifices, and unexpected acts of generosity that made the journey possible.

This is one reason Engineering with a Heart exists. The project stands at the intersection of engineering practice, public explanation, personal memory, and service. It asks how technical work can remain rigorous while also becoming more legible, humane, and meaningful to the communities it ultimately serves.

Guiding themes

  • Engineering excellence should remain connected to human usefulness.
  • Invisible systems deserve clearer public explanation.
  • Storytelling can widen access to technical worlds.
  • Gratitude, dignity, and resilience matter in both life and work.
  • A meaningful career is built not only on performance, but also on service.
