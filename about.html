Journey

A Bridge Across Worlds

Roberto H. Jacob da Silva writes and builds from the perspective of someone who has crossed multiple worlds: Brazil and the United States, scarcity and opportunity, technical rigor and human reflection, private struggle and public contribution. That crossing is not a branding device. It is the lived foundation beneath both Impossible Crossing and Engineering with a Heart.

The project exists in the space where human story, engineering practice, public understanding, and community meaning meet. It grows out of a life shaped by gratitude for family, teachers, mentors, friends, colleagues, and the unexpected acts of generosity that quietly changed what seemed possible.

That is why the work on this site is intentionally hybrid. It does not separate technical excellence from memory, purpose, dignity, or service. It asks what engineering looks like when it remains intellectually serious and deeply human at the same time.

Professional Focus

What He Builds

Roberto’s technical work centers on the architecture, visibility, and diagnosis of complex systems at scale. Across Microsoft, Lenovo, IBM, Nokia, and earlier roles, he has built distributed systems, tracing and telemetry platforms, diagnostics frameworks, networking software, virtualization infrastructure, and reliability tooling for environments where failures are difficult to see and expensive to misunderstand.

His work is strongest where deep systems knowledge meets clarity: making invisible behavior observable, turning massive telemetry into understanding, and helping engineers explain, stabilize, and improve the platforms that quietly power modern life.

Writing and Public Work

What He Writes

His writing moves in two complementary directions. In Impossible Crossing, he tells a life story marked by poverty, migration, education, faith, love, resilience, and the people whose presence changed its direction. In Engineering with a Heart, that same voice expands into reflection on technology, ethics, inequality, service, and the human meaning behind the systems we build.

What gives the writing force is not spectacle, bitterness, or self-congratulation. It is a mature narrative voice that looks at life with gratitude, lucidity, dignity, and unusual emotional restraint. The result is writing that invites readers to walk alongside the journey rather than admire it from a distance.

Creative Life

Music, Voice, and Creative Expression

Music is not an accessory in Roberto’s life. It is one of the languages through which memory, love, longing, courage, and hope have been lived and understood. Across the book, the recordings, and the personal reflections behind each song, music appears as refuge, study companion, emotional archive, family bond, and stage for improbable dreams.

Some songs are tied to decisive chapters of life: Air Supply became fuel during the years of impossible academic ambition; “Goodbye Yellow Brick Road” marks the moment he first dared to sing in public; “Halo” carries the joy of recording with his daughter Gabriela; “A Thousand Years” reflects the love of a father for his daughters; “Never Ending Story” preserves a priceless evening in Recife with relatives now gone. Again and again, music becomes the place where feelings continue after words stop.

That is why this site treats music as a true pillar of identity alongside engineering and the book. Over time, this area can grow into selected recordings, stories behind the songs, playlists, and reflections that show how deeply voice and melody helped shape the journey.

Why this matters here

The emotional core of Impossible Crossing extends naturally into music. The final pages of the book turn into playlists because some chapters are completed not only in prose, but in sound.

For this reason, the site works best when readers can see three connected dimensions at once: the engineer, the author, and the singer.

“This is my story in words. Then it continues in sound.”
